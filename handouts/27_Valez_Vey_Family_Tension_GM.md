# Valez / Vey Family Tension

## Canon update

Dr. Sera Valez and Mara “Mother Red” Vey are estranged sisters.

They were born Sera Valez and Mara Valez. Sera kept the family name and built a life around medicine, legitimacy, and public trust. Mara cut the family name down to **Vey** after prison, Redline initiation, exile, or simply because she refused to carry a respectable name while doing necessary dirty work.

They look similar enough that observant PCs may notice it quickly.

Do not treat this as a hidden “gotcha.” It is a pressure point, a social clue, and a way to make the neighborhood politics more personal.

## Core dynamic

Sera and Mara protect the same people through incompatible methods.

```text
Sera protects people through medicine, legitimacy, records, triage, and public moral authority.
Mara protects people through territory, fear, smuggling, favors, retaliation, and informal power.
```

Neither sister is entirely wrong.

Neither sister wants the PCs to weaponize the relationship in public.

## Public truth

Most locals know there is history between Dr. Valez and Mother Red, but not everyone knows exactly what it is.

Children and old residents may speak more freely:

```text
“Auntie Red paid for breakfast again.”
“Don’t call her that where grown-ups hear.”
“Why?”
“Because she gets mad when people know she’s nice.”
```

“Auntie Red” is partly community slang and partly literal family residue. Mara is not everyone’s aunt, but she has acted like one often enough that children use the name.

## What Sera says

Sera does not deny the relationship if directly and respectfully confronted, but she refuses gossip.

Possible lines:

> “Mara is my sister. She is also the reason half these children ate last month. She is also the reason three men came in with broken ribs. Both things are true.”

> “Do not use my family to start a war in my clinic.”

> “I heal the people who make it through my doors. Mara keeps some of them alive long enough to reach those doors. That does not make her methods clean.”

## What Mara says

Mara hates sounding sentimental. She deflects first.

Possible lines:

> “Sera saves people who make it through her doors. I save the ones who never get that far.”

> “She thinks I chose this. I think she chose a room with windows and called it virtue.”

> “Say ‘sister’ like it gives you leverage and I will show you what leverage is.”

## How to reveal it

Use any of these:

- A child calls Mara “Auntie Red.”
- Sera and Mara argue with the rhythm of people who have done it since childhood.
- Sister Luma refers to “the Valez girls” before correcting herself.
- Mara knows old clinic maintenance details she should not know.
- Sera knows Redline aid routes but pretends she does not.
- A family photo in a clinic back room shows two similar young women, one with Sera’s name and one with Mara’s old surname.
- Talla “Auntie’s Eyes” Vey uses “Vey” because Mara took her in, not because they are blood relatives.

## How it affects negotiations

The relationship should create routes, not close them.

### If PCs respect both sisters

- Sera may ask Mara for evacuation help.
- Mara may order Redline to protect clinic corridors.
- Both may agree to keep the relay in clinic custody temporarily.
- Redline aid routes can become evacuation routes.
- The sisters can coordinate without admitting reconciliation.

### If PCs expose the relationship mockingly

- Mara becomes hostile.
- Sera shuts down personal questions.
- Redline underlings interpret it as an attack on Mara’s reputation.
- Children and civilians become less willing to talk.

### If PCs try to blackmail either sister

- It should backfire.
- The community may resent outsiders exploiting a family wound.
- Corporate Recovery may learn the link and use it to pressure both factions.

## How Corporate Recovery can use it

Commander Rusk may not know the relationship at first.

Orlan Pike may discover it through surveillance and try to exploit it:

```text
Leak the relationship to make Sera look gang-compromised.
Frame Redline aid as clinic corruption.
Suggest Mara is using the clinic as a shield.
Pressure Sera with “your sister’s criminal network.”
Pressure Mara with “your sister’s medical license.”
```

This gives the spy subplot teeth.

## How the hidden nest changes the relationship

Once the second nest is proven, the sisters’ conflict becomes secondary.

The best scene is not a reconciliation speech. It is a tactical compromise:

```text
Sera: “I need patients out.”
Mara: “I need your back doors open.”
Sera: “No weapons in recovery.”
Mara: “No uniforms near the children.”
Sera: “Done.”
Mara: “Done.”
```

They still disagree. They still move.

## GM use

Use this relationship to make social scenes sharper:

- Sera can veto violence in the clinic.
- Mara can unlock routes, lookouts, and aid networks.
- Either sister can force the other to act if civilians are in danger.
- Corporate exploitation of the family tie can reveal the spy/handler plot.
- The PCs can become trusted only by proving they are not trying to own the sisters’ history.
