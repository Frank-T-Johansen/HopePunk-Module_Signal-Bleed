## Choosing a pregen

Pick a pregen by choosing a Background, then ask the GM to move that character from the **Pregens** folder to the **Characters** folder and assign it to you.

The character names are written as:

```text
Character Name (Background)
```

The part in parentheses is only there to make pregens easier to pick.

## Customizing before play

You may customize the pregen before play:

### 1. Rename the character

The name and handle are only suggestions. You may remove the “(Background)” part from the character name after choosing the pregen.

You may also change the portrait/token if you want.

### 2. Move starting skill points

Each pregen has two extra starting skill points already spent. These are shown as skills with 2 points.

You may move one or both of those extra points:

- remove 1 point from a skill that has 2 points
- add that point to any other skill that currently has 0 or 1 point

Do not reduce a Background skill below 1 if it was part of the pregen’s starting Background package, unless the GM allows a larger rebuild.

### 3. Move proficiencies

Each pregen has two proficiencies.

You may move either proficiency to any skill that has at least 1 point after your skill changes.

This means you may first move a skill point into a 0-point skill, making it 1, and then move a proficiency to that newly chosen skill.

### 4. Spend money

You may spend remaining money to buy additional gear. See the rulebook for what is available.

### 5. Keep the Background

The Background, Background Ability, and starting gear are part of the pregen’s chosen Background package.

Ask the GM before changing those.

## Starting situation

You are at **Mercy Twelve**, a community clinic in a neglected district.

You were hired, asked, pressured, or personally drawn into a simple protection job:

> Keep the peace for one evening. Protect the clinic. Do not let a damaged relay/data-core leave until it has been checked. Avoid bloodshed if possible.

It should have been simple.
