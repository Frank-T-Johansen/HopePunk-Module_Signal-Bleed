# Map D — Quarantine, Incident Floor, and Landing Corner Key

Use this as the likely breach and finale floor. It includes a landing corner for NightCrash or emergency craft, but most of the map remains part of the indoor megacomplex.

## How to use these checks

These are prompts, not a fixed menu. If a player describes a sensible approach, use the closest skill or Background logic.

Recommended clue handling:

- **No roll / basic interaction:** give the obvious clue.
- **Success:** give useful detail, leverage, or a safer route.
- **High success:** give a shortcut, future advantage, or a way to reduce danger.
- **Failure:** do not stall the scenario; give the clue with less detail, extra time cost, noise, or complication.

## D1. Landing Corner / Skywell Pad

**Read-aloud:**

A small landing pad opens to a vertical skywell between megacomplex towers. Warning lights pulse against the ceiling haze.

**GM notes:**

This is where NightCrash can arrive. It is a small exposed corner, not an outdoor map.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Piloting:** Judge whether a fast emergency craft can land safely.
- **Awareness:** Spot incoming lights or drone movement before arrival.
- **Corporate Security:** Recognize emergency access protocols.
- **Performance:** Use a public arrival to change morale.

**Useful information:**

- NightCrash can arrive here if needed.
- A flying vehicle cannot solve the whole facility problem.

## D2. Skywell Access Door

**Read-aloud:**

A reinforced door connects the landing corner to the facility interior. It is built to keep weather, smoke, and panic out.

**GM notes:**

A chokepoint for rescue or retreat.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Grease Monkey:** Force the door safely.
- **Mesh Hacker:** Override emergency lockout.
- **Big Guns:** Know what would happen if someone breached it the loud way.
- **Athletics:** Hold or force it during pressure.

**Useful information:**

- This door may trap or save people depending on timing.
- NightCrash’s drones need it clear for evacuation.

## D3. Central Incident Hall

**Read-aloud:**

A wide hall links containment, diagnostics, patient holding, and vertical access. It feels too open when the lights start flickering.

**GM notes:**

This is the natural action scene space. It should change based on earlier diplomacy.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Tactics:** Set defensive positions without blocking evacuation.
- **Small Arms:** Identify clean firing lanes and dangerous crossfire.
- **Performance / Leadership:** Direct panicked civilians or staff.
- **Parkour:** Use furniture and barriers to move around the fight.

**Useful information:**

- This can become a kill zone or an evacuation corridor.
- Earlier faction choices determine who helps hold it.

## D4. Quarantine Cells

**Read-aloud:**

Pink-lit quarantine rooms line the corridor. Each cell has observation glass, isolation seals, and equipment meant to keep fear contained.

**GM notes:**

Use for exposed patients, alien symptoms, or corporate containment cruelty.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine:** Distinguish infection, signal exposure, and panic.
- **Psychology:** Calm an isolated patient enough to get useful information.
- **Corporate Security:** Recognize the layout as containment-first, care-second.
- **Blades / Melee:** Notice defensive gouges from someone trapped inside.

**Useful information:**

- The quarantine system can protect or imprison.
- A patient may know what triggered the breach.

## D5. Diagnostics Lab

**Read-aloud:**

Diagnostic beds, scanners, and monitors surround a central work area. Several screens show clean error states that feel too clean.

**GM notes:**

This is where technical/medical PCs can learn what the relay really is.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine / Surgery:** Interpret physiological effects of signal exposure.
- **Mesh Hacker:** Recover corrupted diagnostic logs.
- **Cyberware:** Notice implants respond like antennae, not just damaged electronics.
- **Investigation:** Catch that the lab results were sanitized by an automated routine.

**Useful information:**

- The relay is a lure or beacon, not just data storage.
- Exposure can trigger Samurai potential.

## D6. Control Room

**Read-aloud:**

Curved consoles face camera feeds, isolation controls, air systems, and security doors. Half the indicators are teal. The others pulse wrong.

**GM notes:**

This is the room for map control, door choices, and hard moral tradeoffs.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Mesh Hacker:** Control doors, cameras, or containment fields.
- **Corporate Security:** Predict recovery override procedures.
- **Grease Monkey:** Keep controls running when software fails.
- **Tactics:** Choose which doors to lock without trapping civilians.

**Useful information:**

- The PCs can shape the battlefield from here.
- Bad decisions may trap allies or spread the breach.

## D7. Patient Holding Rooms

**Read-aloud:**

Small rooms hold beds, monitors, and privacy curtains. They are not cells, but they are not comforting either.

**GM notes:**

Useful for civilian rescue, Bluewire treatment, or exposed NPCs.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine:** Stabilize a patient while alarms rise.
- **Empathy:** Get a terrified person to move now instead of freeze.
- **Athletics:** Carry someone to the elevator or landing route.
- **Religious / Comfort:** Help someone face impossible symptoms without despair.

**Useful information:**

- The players should feel the cost of delays here.
- Saving patients can matter more than killing enemies.

## D8. Containment Chamber

**Read-aloud:**

A sealed chamber holds the relay, or what the relay has become. Light bends wrong near the center.

**GM notes:**

Primary breach point candidate. Do not make the solution one obvious roll.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Mesh Hacker:** Stabilize signal output temporarily.
- **Grease Monkey:** Physically isolate power or shielding.
- **Samurai Otaku:** Recognize patterns from rumored Samurai telemetry.
- **Big Guns:** Judge whether destroying the chamber will help or make everything worse.
- **Psychology:** Notice the signal reacts to fear, pain, or attention.

**Useful information:**

- The relay is actively interacting with the environment.
- Broadcast/destroy/delay choices should have consequences.

## D9. Service Bypass

**Read-aloud:**

A narrow corridor of pipes, access panels, and yellow service lights runs behind the main rooms.

**GM notes:**

Use for alternate entry, stealth, flanking, or alien movement.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Stealth:** Move around the main hall unseen.
- **Scavenger:** Find useful tools or shortcuts.
- **Gutter Rat:** Know which panel leads to a maintenance crawl.
- **Melee Combat:** Understand the danger of close fighting in the narrow space.

**Useful information:**

- This route can save the party if the central hall is overrun.
- It can also let something bypass them.

## D10. Elevator Core

**Read-aloud:**

The elevator doors shudder with each pulse. The floor indicator flickers between valid floors and symbols that are not floors.

**GM notes:**

Dangerous vertical connection. Great tension point.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Mesh Hacker:** Stop the elevator carrying the breach to other floors.
- **Grease Monkey:** Force it open or hold it shut mechanically.
- **Tactics:** Decide whether to sacrifice mobility for containment.
- **Awareness:** Hear whether something is inside before opening it.

**Useful information:**

- The elevator is useful but risky.
- Alien effects can spread through vertical infrastructure.

## D11. Emergency Stair / Ladderwell

**Read-aloud:**

The emergency shaft drops into red-lit gloom. Its metal steps are narrow, steep, and echo too long.

**GM notes:**

Last-resort route. Good for escape or horror pressure.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Athletics:** Move fast under pressure.
- **Parkour:** Skip landings or bypass obstacles.
- **Awareness:** Detect movement from a lower floor.
- **Small Arms:** Know firing here risks ricochets and panic.

**Useful information:**

- Useful if elevators are compromised.
- Bad for stretchers and mass evacuation.

## D12. NightCrash Arrival Route

**Read-aloud:**

From landing pad to hall, the route is just wide enough for two nurse-shaped drones carrying a smart-stretcher at a dead run.

**GM notes:**

This route matters only if NightCrash appears or if PCs prepare evacuation.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Logistics / Piloting:** Keep the route clear for medevac.
- **Athletics:** Move debris or bodies out of the way.
- **Performance / Leadership:** Make frightened people stop blocking the path.
- **Small Arms:** Cover the route without endangering patients.

**Useful information:**

- NightCrash creates an opening, not a solution.
- If the route is blocked, her help is delayed or reduced.

## Updated old-nest and relay clues for Map D

Map D should represent the old quarantine/incident layer, corporate records, and the first nest evidence. It is not necessarily where the current hidden nest is.

### D3 Central Incident Hall

- Signs of old corporate sterilization: scorch, foam, sealed doors, chemical burns.
- Big Guns or Corporate Security can identify a professional cleanup assault.
- Medicine can identify that some stains are biological remains, not ordinary blood.

### D5 Diagnostics Lab

- Exposure-treatment records.
- Patient trial data connected to Mercy Twelve.
- False discharge or transfer paperwork.
- Evidence that locals were used as deniable subjects.

### D6 Control Room

- Cleanup footage of the first nest.
- Model 1s are seen flying away in formation.
- Corporate annotation: “terminal erratic flight.”
- Halden Rook’s annotation: “They’re not fleeing. They’re relocating.”
- A deleted risk tag: “nest-seeding possibility.”
- This is the best place for the deep clue that the second nest may exist.

### D8 Containment Chamber

- The visible first nest was here or adjacent.
- It is nearly destroyed.
- Researchers were consumed as biomass.
- Corporate Recovery thinks this was the only nest.
- The chamber can be horrific evidence, but should not be the main current threat unless the GM wants a second combat site.

### D9 Service Bypass

- This route may match the Model 1 escape vector.
- Tracking / Investigation can connect it to lower service infrastructure.
- It points toward Map C rather than ending the mystery on Map D.
