# Map A — Mercy Twelve Clinic and Indoor Street Key

Use this key for the primary clinic floor. The purpose of these prompts is not to hide mandatory clues behind single checks, but to encourage players to interact with the environment using many different skills.

For a starter session, give the basic clue freely if the players investigate the right thing. Rolls should improve detail, reveal leverage, reduce danger, or create an advantage.

## How to use these checks

These are prompts, not a fixed menu. If a player describes a sensible approach, use the closest skill or Background logic.

Recommended clue handling:

- **No roll / basic interaction:** give the obvious clue.
- **Success:** give useful detail, leverage, or a safer route.
- **High success:** give a shortcut, future advantage, or a way to reduce danger.
- **Failure:** do not stall the scenario; give the clue with less detail, extra time cost, noise, or complication.

## A1. Indoor Street / Public Concourse

**Read-aloud:**

A broad indoor street runs past Mercy Twelve: benches, planters, food kiosks, flickering ad-panels, and pedestrians who pretend not to stare at the clinic doors.

**GM notes:**

This is public space. Gang lookouts, clinic volunteers, corporate watchers, and ordinary megacomplex residents can all plausibly be here.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Streetwise / Local Knowledge:** Identify who belongs here and who is pretending to belong.
- **Awareness:** Spot a corporate observer using a kiosk reflection instead of looking directly.
- **Performance:** Draw a crowd, calm a crowd, or make public escalation costly.
- **Small Arms:** Notice several bystanders are carrying cheap concealed pistols badly, more fear than intent.
- **Bounty Hunter / Tracking:** Pick out the same courier-route scuff marks leading toward the clinic entrance.

**Useful information:**

- The clinic is watched from several directions.
- Most locals want the clinic left alone.
- Public violence here will have social consequences.

## A2. Public Front Entrance

**Read-aloud:**

Double glass doors open into the public clinic side. The doors are reinforced, but covered in old stickers, children’s drawings, and hand-written notices.

**GM notes:**

This is the normal civilian route. It is not designed for emergency gurney traffic.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Corporate Security:** Recognize that the entrance is hard to storm cleanly but easy to intimidate.
- **Grease Monkey / Repair:** Spot that the door motors have been repaired with off-brand parts.
- **Social / Empathy:** Read the volunteer at the door as exhausted, not hostile.
- **Mesh Hacker:** Notice the door access log has a six-minute gap from earlier tonight.

**Useful information:**

- The courier did not arrive through the public entrance.
- Someone has been tampering with access logs.
- The door can be locked down, but that may trap civilians.

## A3. Reception and Waiting Area

**Read-aloud:**

The waiting area is crowded, tense, and too bright. Patients sit beside gang lookouts, tired parents, and people who clearly came here because there was nowhere else to go.

**GM notes:**

This is the first social pressure cooker. Bluewire may pass through, but should not start here unless the GM wants immediate escalation.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Psychology / Empathy:** Separate fear, pain, anger, and loyalty in the crowd.
- **Performance:** Distract the waiting room long enough for staff to move patients.
- **Medicine:** Identify which patients are urgent and which are frightened but stable.
- **Blades / Melee Combat:** Notice who is posturing for intimidation and who is actually ready to lunge.
- **Religious / Community angle:** Comfort someone who thinks the signal pulse was a bad omen.

**Useful information:**

- The crowd can become an obstacle or an ally.
- Positive, direct reassurance works better here than threats.
- Several people know rumors about Mara’s hidden support, but will not volunteer them casually.

## A4. Pediatric / Community Corner

**Read-aloud:**

A bright corner of soft mats, worn toys, wall murals, cheap tablets, and half-finished homework. It is painfully out of place beside armed adults.

**GM notes:**

Use this to underline stakes. Keet or another young witness can be found here.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Student Dropout / Education:** Notice the lesson material is current despite the neighborhood’s poverty.
- **Piss-Poor Artist / Performance:** Read the murals as community-made, with hidden initials from local kids.
- **Investigation:** Find signs that a child saw the courier arrive from an unexpected direction.
- **Social / Empathy:** Get a child to explain what adults refuse to say.
- **Small Arms:** Notice someone deliberately chose not to fire toward this corner earlier.

**Useful information:**

- The community support is organized, not accidental.
- Mara’s people may have supplied school materials.
- Children saw more of the courier incident than adults realize.

## A5. Triage Desk

**Read-aloud:**

A curved desk forms the clinic’s nerve center: patient slates, scanner feeds, cheap coffee, half-empty medfoam canisters, and staff who have not slept enough.

**GM notes:**

Dr. Valez or a senior nurse can appear here. This is a good place for PCs to earn trust quickly.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine:** Prioritize patients and win staff confidence.
- **Cyberware / Mesh:** Notice local devices pulse in sync with the relay.
- **Corporate Security:** Recognize the desk has no defense against a formal corporate seizure order.
- **Persuasion:** Convince staff to share restricted information.
- **Grease Monkey:** Notice power dips are coming from deeper infrastructure, not just bad wiring.

**Useful information:**

- The relay is interfering with patient devices.
- Staff are hiding details to prevent panic.
- The clinic needs help more than it needs muscle.

## A6. Emergency Intake Bay

**Read-aloud:**

The non-public emergency bay smells of disinfectant, hot battery cells, and old panic. A vehicle bay connects directly to trauma access.

**GM notes:**

This is how patients should arrive by ambulance or stretcher. The courier did not use this route.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine / Surgery:** Confirm the bay is prepared for trauma work, not routine intake.
- **Small Arms:** Identify low-caliber warning-shot damage near the outer shutter.
- **Big Guns:** Recognize the shutters are reinforced against breaching weapons.
- **Mesh Hacker:** Recover partial intake logs or notice deleted camera metadata.
- **Grease Monkey:** Spot that the parked emergency vehicle battery was drained recently.
- **Streetwise:** Know this entrance is watched by both clinic volunteers and Redline Choir lookouts.

**Useful information:**

- The courier arrived from inside the megacomplex, not by ambulance.
- The emergency bay cameras were looped.
- The bay is the best route for evacuating critical patients.

## A7. Trauma / Surgery Rooms

**Read-aloud:**

Bright surgical light pools over clean tables, patched machines, bundled cables, and carefully rationed sterile supplies.

**GM notes:**

This is where the relay-exposed patient or courier may be treated. It should feel precious and vulnerable.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Surgery:** Understand what treatment is possible and what resources are missing.
- **Cyber-Junkie / Cyberware:** Feel that the signal is irritating implants and medical devices.
- **Investigation:** Notice someone moved a critical device recently and cleaned poorly afterward.
- **Blades:** Read defensive cuts or close-quarters trauma on a patient.
- **Rogue Surgeon:** Identify illegal but effective emergency modifications in the equipment.

**Useful information:**

- The relay is medically dangerous.
- Someone tried to hide how bad the exposure symptoms are.
- The clinic can stabilize people, but not fight a prolonged breach here.

## A8. Pharmacy and Supply

**Read-aloud:**

Locked shelves hold antibiotics, painkillers, medfoam, cheap nutrition packs, and labeled boxes whose labels do not match the contents.

**GM notes:**

This room can reveal Mara’s covert support and also tempt desperate characters.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine:** Identify which supplies are lifesaving and which are merely valuable.
- **Streetwise:** Recognize Redline Choir smuggling marks on supposedly anonymous crates.
- **Investigation:** Find a hidden ledger of food, medicine, and school supply deliveries.
- **Sleight / Cat Burglar angle:** Open a cabinet without triggering staff alarms.
- **Social:** Convince a volunteer to admit who paid for recent stock.

**Useful information:**

- Mara has been quietly funding medicine and food.
- The clinic is one supply disruption away from collapse.
- Exposing Mara’s good works publicly may damage her protective image.

## A9. Recovery Wing

**Read-aloud:**

Small resting rooms line a quieter corridor: beds, thin blankets, privacy curtains, small plants, and monitors running at minimum power.

**GM notes:**

This is where civilian vulnerability is most visible. Do not route all movement through reception; staff need internal corridors to bring patients here from trauma.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Medicine:** Notice which patients cannot be moved quickly.
- **Empathy:** Calm a patient enough to describe strange symptoms.
- **Awareness:** Spot that one recovery room has been watched from the corridor.
- **Piloting / Logistics:** Plan an evacuation path using elevators and wide corridors.
- **Athletics:** Move a patient safely under time pressure without a proper gurney.

**Useful information:**

- Evacuation will be difficult if the elevator locks down.
- Some patients are already reacting to the signal.
- The recovery wing gives players a reason not to let fighting spill into the clinic.

## A10. Staff Office / Records

**Read-aloud:**

A cramped office of battered filing cabinets, cheap terminals, mugs, old protest posters, and a locked drawer everyone pretends not to notice.

**GM notes:**

Use this for deeper investigation, corp records, deleted logs, or proof of the clinic/gang relationship.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Mesh Hacker:** Recover deleted access logs.
- **Corporate Security:** Recognize the format of a corporate seizure notice before it is officially served.
- **Investigation:** Find mismatched patient records connected to the relay.
- **Persuasion:** Convince staff to reveal what Dr. Valez is withholding.
- **Forgery / Admin:** Create a temporary record that buys time against corp recovery.

**Useful information:**

- The corp has legal pretext prepared.
- The relay case predates tonight.
- The clinic is trying to verify the evidence, not hide it forever.

## A11. Elevator Core

**Read-aloud:**

A heavy elevator core hums behind teal-lit doors. It is large enough for beds, carts, and evac drones.

**GM notes:**

This is the safe vertical route. It is also hackable, lockable, and vulnerable to signal interference.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Grease Monkey:** Diagnose power strain and prevent a stall.
- **Mesh Hacker:** Override floor access or detect a remote lockout.
- **Corporate Security:** Predict how recovery teams would seize elevator control.
- **Piloting / Logistics:** Coordinate patient movement under pressure.
- **Big Guns:** Recognize why this is a bad place to be trapped if enemies breach doors.

**Useful information:**

- The elevator can connect all active maps.
- If corrupted, it can spread danger between floors.
- It is the best route for the Gurney Angels.

## A12. Emergency Stair / Ladderwell

**Read-aloud:**

A narrow vertical access shaft holds steep metal stairs, ladder-sections, and emergency lighting that flickers with every signal pulse.

**GM notes:**

If the map art looks ladder-like, describe this as a compact megacomplex stair/ladderwell. Good for PCs, bad for stretchers.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Parkour / Athletics:** Move quickly without slipping or blocking others.
- **Stealth:** Use the shaft to bypass public areas.
- **Grease Monkey:** Notice old maintenance access panels along the shaft.
- **Melee Combat:** Understand how bad a fight here would be.
- **Gutter Rat / Scavenger:** Know which landings connect to unofficial routes.

**Useful information:**

- This is a bypass if the elevator is locked.
- Moving wounded civilians here is slow and risky.
- A chase can move vertically through this shaft.

## A13. Service Corridor

**Read-aloud:**

A narrow back corridor links supply, staff, maintenance, and emergency access without crossing the waiting room.

**GM notes:**

This is important for logical hospital movement. It is also a quiet place for tense confrontations.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Awareness:** Hear voices before opening the next door.
- **Small Arms:** Identify the best defensive angles without firing.
- **Streetwise:** Recognize Redline Choir movement patterns.
- **Corporate Security:** Spot where a recovery team would stack up before breach.
- **Medicine:** Plan a patient route from trauma to recovery.

**Useful information:**

- Staff can move patients internally.
- Gang and corp factions both want control of this corridor.
- It can become the main evacuation route.

## A14. Bluewire’s Pacing Route

**Read-aloud:**

The floor near one corridor junction is scuffed by repeated boot turns. Someone has been pacing there hard enough to leave marks.

**GM notes:**

This is a mobile keyed location. Place it where Bluewire has been spiraling near the restricted or emergency area.

**Interaction prompts:**

- **Psychology:** Read agitation, fear, and overstimulation from the pacing pattern.
- **Medicine:** Connect sweating, tremor, and pain to withdrawal or opioid dependence.
- **Cyberware:** Notice his ware is running hot and reacting to signal pulses.
- **Melee Combat:** Predict that he is more likely to lunge than aim carefully.
- **Positive social approach:** Use his name and give him a face-saving task; this should matter more than clever diagnosis alone.

**Useful information:**

- Bluewire is obvious to read; this is not a hidden mystery.
- Difficulty should be low: 8 to notice serious distress, 10 to identify likely dependence/cyber-strain.
- Encouragement and respect can have a huge stabilizing effect.
