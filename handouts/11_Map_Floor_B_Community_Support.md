# Map: Floor B — Repurposed Recovery & Support Ward

## Function

Floor B is a mixed recovery and support level that Mercy Twelve has reclaimed from an older, sealed clinical wing.

On the public side, it still supports the neighborhood: short-stay recovery, family support, food aid, counselling, temporary safe rooms, staff intake interviews, and civilian evacuation staging.

On the sealed side, it carries the scars of something older and much worse.

B8 is not just an isolation ward. It was temporarily converted into a covert corporate research and quarantine space. That operation failed. Corporate containment forces later burned and cleaned the area, but not perfectly.

Use this floor to connect three parts of the scenario:

1. why the neighborhood depends on Mercy Twelve,
2. why Mara’s hidden support network matters,
3. and how the old corporate Antithesis experiment left evidence behind.

## What the map shows

Key areas:

- upper public / service walkway
- procedure and extended-care rooms
- records and monitoring equipment
- staff intake or quiet consultation rooms
- support corridor
- short-stay recovery and counselling rooms
- sealed isolation ward
- elevator / lift access
- stairwell / vertical access
- lower exterior / service access
- staff rest and overnight support room

The exact room labels should follow the visual map. If an old key calls B9 “counselor offices” but the map clearly shows an elevator or lift core there, use the map: B9 is lift access, not an office.

## Scenario use

Good scenes here:

- PCs discover that Mara secretly funds meals, medicine, and school support.
- Children or volunteers reveal “Auntie Red” without understanding the political danger.
- Keet gives information about the courier or signal pulses.
- Sister Luma helps calm witnesses or traumatized patients from a quiet support room near B6.
- Civilians complicate any simple fight with Redline or corporate forces.
- PCs discover signs that B8 was used for illegal Antithesis research.
- The recovered notebook points from the old ward disaster toward the escaped Model 1s and the later hidden nest.

## Mara’s hidden support

This is still one of the best floors for discovering that Mara “Mother Red” Vey is not only running a gang.

Possible clues:

- food crates that came through Redline Choir channels,
- medicine and meal packs paid for through anonymous street accounts,
- school supplies routed through cutouts,
- volunteers who go quiet when Mara is mentioned,
- children who call her “Auntie Red,”
- and a ledger showing food, medicine, generator fuel, and support stipends.

This should not be public knowledge.

If the PCs discover it respectfully, Mara becomes easier to negotiate with. If they expose it to mock her or weaken her reputation, she reacts badly.

## B8: sealed isolation ward / old experiment site

B8 should feel different from the rest of the floor.

The public support spaces are patched together, underfunded, and human. B8 is colder, harder, and partially erased.

Visible signs:

- scorch marks under fresh wall coating,
- mismatched replacement panels,
- melted medical equipment,
- doors gouged from the inside,
- sterilization residue,
- patched drainage grates,
- dead camera angles,
- and service gaps that were sealed after something had already escaped.

This was the original local containment disaster. Corporate forces believe they destroyed the primary nest here. They are mostly right about B8 and dangerously wrong about the wider threat.

The key clue is not “the nest is still here.”

The key clue is:

> Something escaped from here.

## Discoverable evidence in B8

Use these checks as prompts, not a fixed menu.

- **Awareness / Investigation:** Notice scorch marks under fresh sealant, mismatched replacement panels, or cleanup patterns that hide a larger violent event.
- **Medicine / Surgery:** Identify tissue damage, triage failure, and biological contamination inconsistent with ordinary quarantine work.
- **Xenobiology / Antithesis knowledge:** Recognize that the notes describe live Antithesis growth, not inert sample storage.
- **Big Guns / Corporate Security / Ex-Military:** Identify powered-armor entry damage, incendiary cleanup patterns, and professional evidence destruction.
- **Mesh Hacker / Security:** Recover missing camera timestamps, deleted local logs, or evidence that the ward cameras were isolated from normal clinic systems.
- **Grease Monkey / Scavenger:** Find the loose panel hiding the recovered notebook.

Failure should not block the clue. On a failure, give the clue with a cost: extra time, noise, a hostile camera ping, a frightened witness, or a Model 3 movement tick elsewhere.

## Main scene beats

1. PCs see that the support ward keeps vulnerable locals alive.
2. Children, volunteers, or ledgers reveal that Mara’s network funds real aid.
3. Lala’s disappearance connects food aid to service routes.
4. Narin’s disappearance shows how vulnerable people vanish without official response.
5. B8 reveals that the corporation’s “old contained incident” was an illegal experiment and cleanup.
6. The notebook confirms that Model 1s escaped the first nest.
7. The PCs realize the missing-person pattern may be the result of a second hidden nest, not leftover panic from the first disaster.

## Camera ownership

- Public support areas: community, municipal, or ad-network cameras.
- Pantry and service routes: Redline-influenced or locally patched feeds.
- Recovery, counselling, and shelter areas: restricted for privacy; use door logs instead of ordinary camera coverage.
- B8 isolation ward: local isolated camera system, heavily deleted or physically destroyed.
- Lift and stair access: partial logs, unreliable during lockdown.

## What this floor should establish

- Mara’s power has a protective side.
- Mercy Twelve is more than a clinic.
- The missing people are locals the system can ignore.
- The corporation’s original experiment happened under deniable medical cover.
- The first nest was burned out, but not before Model 1s escaped.
- The present danger is not in B8 anymore; B8 points toward the hidden second nest.
