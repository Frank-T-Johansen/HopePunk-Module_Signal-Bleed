# Bluewire: Distress, Cyber Strain, and De-escalation

## GM principle

Bluewire is obviously unwell.

The point is not to trick the players. The point is to reward them for paying attention to health, psychology, pain, and fear before violence starts.

## What the PCs can see

Bluewire is shaking, sweating, scanning exits, clenching and unclenching his cybernetic hand, and reacting half a second too late to words but instantly to sudden movement.

He stands too close to doors. Nobody stands behind him. Other gang members give him space without saying why.

He is trying to look dangerous because he feels terrified.

## Suggested difficulties

Use the system’s normal check structure. The exact skill depends on the character’s approach.

**Difficulty 8:** Notice he is unstable, frightened, overstimulated, and in pain.

**Difficulty 10:** Identify likely opioid dependence, withdrawal symptoms, cyber-strain overload, and emotional dysregulation.

**Difficulty 12:** Realize the damaged signal/device is aggravating his implants.

**Difficulty 14:** Work out a safe de-escalation plan: reduce stimulation, give him space, speak directly but calmly, offer a face-saving exit, involve someone he trusts, or get medical sedation ready.

**Difficulty 16:** Predict his next trigger before it happens.

## Helpful approaches

- use his name
- lower your voice
- give him physical space
- offer a concrete choice
- acknowledge pain without pity
- let him save face
- move bystanders away
- reduce noise/light/stimulation
- ask what he needs right now
- give him a useful task that does not put civilians at risk

## Impact

If a PC sincerely treats Bluewire as a person instead of a threat, reduce the next social/medical check involving him by 2.

If a PC publicly gives him a face-saving way to stand down, reduce the difficulty by 4.

If a PC mocks him, corners him, calls him a junkie, or tries to humiliate him, increase the difficulty by 4 and advance escalation.
