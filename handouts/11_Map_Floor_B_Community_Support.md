# Map: Floor B — Community Support, Shelter, and School Annex

## Function

This floor shows the civilian support infrastructure tied to Mercy Twelve.

It is not just “extra rooms.” It explains why people care about the clinic and why the Redline Choir and Dr. Valez both see the place as politically important.

## What the map shows

Key areas:

- indoor streets around the support center
- public entrance
- community commons
- pantry / aid distribution point
- cafeteria or communal kitchen
- classroom / workshop room
- children’s corner or daycare room
- counselor / caseworker offices
- dormitory / shelter sleeping area
- secure back/service corridor
- elevator core
- emergency stair / ladderwell

## Scenario use

Good scenes here:

- players discover Mara secretly funds meals and school support
- children recognize a PC or NightCrash brand
- Keet gives information about the courier
- civilians hide during the breach
- gang members guard a food delivery
- clinic staff ask PCs to help move patients or kids
- social consequences become visible

## Mara’s hidden support

This is the best map for discovering that Mara “Mother Red” Vey is not just running a gang.

Possible clues:

- food crates that came through Redline Choir channels
- school supplies paid for through anonymous street accounts
- teachers who go quiet when Mara is mentioned
- children who call her “Auntie Red”
- a ledger showing meal packs, medicine, generator fuel, and teacher stipends

This should not be public knowledge. If the PCs discover it respectfully, Mara becomes easier to negotiate with. If they expose it to mock her or weaken her image, she becomes angry.

## Civilian stakes

Use this floor to make the mission about more than the relay.

If the PCs fail, these are the people who suffer:

- patients
- schoolkids
- volunteers
- hungry families
- night-shift workers
- people with nowhere else to sleep

## Elevator and stair notes

The elevator can move food carts, stretchers, and evacuation groups.

The stair/ladderwell can move small groups but should be slower and more dangerous under panic conditions.

## What happens on this map

This floor makes the neighborhood worth saving. Use it for schoolchildren, food aid, Mara’s hidden support network, missing-person reports, and civilian evacuation pressure.

### Camera ownership

- Public areas: community/municipal/ad-network.
- Pantry/service corridor: Redline-influenced.
- Children and shelter areas: restricted for privacy.
- Counselor offices: no ordinary camera coverage; only door logs.

### What this floor should establish

- Mara’s power has a protective side.
- Missing people are locals the system can ignore.
- The missing-person trail is not random.
- The nest is feeding on people with quiet routes and weak official protection.
