# Map: Floor B — Repurposed Recovery & Support Ward

## Function

Floor B was originally built as a clinical overflow and recovery ward, but Mercy Twelve has repurposed it into a mixed support level.

It now handles short-stay recovery, family support, harm-reduction counselling, temporary safe rooms, staff intake interviews, and sealed isolation cases that cannot safely remain on the public clinic floor.

The result is visibly half-hospital, half-community refuge: medical beds, monitoring stations, and sealed rooms sit beside improvised counselling offices, overnight support spaces, aid storage, and staff work areas.

This floor explains why people care about Mercy Twelve and why both Dr. Valez and Mara “Mother Red” Vey see the site as politically important. The clinic is not only treating wounds. It is keeping fragile community infrastructure alive.

Good scenes here:

- players discover Mara secretly funds meals, recovery beds, and emergency support
- children or patients recognize a PC or NightCrash brand
- Keet gives information about the courier

- clinic staff ask PCs to help move patients, supplies, or kids
- Redline members guard aid deliveries through service routes
- social consequences become visible
- the Antithesis missing-person trail becomes personal

## Mara’s hidden support

This is the best map for discovering that Mara “Mother Red” Vey is not just running a gang.

Possible clues:

- food crates and medical supplies that came through Redline Choir channels
- short-stay recovery beds kept available by anonymous street accounts
- teachers, counsellors, or volunteers who go quiet when Mara is mentioned
- children who call her “Auntie Red”
- a ledger showing meal packs, medicine, generator fuel, teacher stipends, and patient-support costs

This should not be public knowledge. If the PCs discover it respectfully, Mara becomes easier to negotiate with. If they expose it to mock her or weaken her image, she becomes angry.

## Civilian stakes

Use this floor to make the mission about more than the relay. If the PCs fail, these are the people who suffer:

- short-stay patients
- schoolkids
- volunteers
- hungry families
- night-shift workers
- people with nowhere else to sleep
- patients who cannot safely use ordinary public services

## Elevator and stair notes

The elevator can move food carts, stretchers, and evacuation groups. The stair/ladderwell can move small groups but should be slower and more dangerous under panic conditions.

## What happens on this map

This floor makes the neighborhood worth saving. Use it for schoolchildren, food aid, Mara’s hidden support network, missing-person reports, vulnerable patients, and civilian evacuation pressure.
