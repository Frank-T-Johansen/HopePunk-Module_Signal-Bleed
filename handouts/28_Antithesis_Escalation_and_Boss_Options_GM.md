# Antithesis Escalation and Boss Options

## Purpose

Signal Bleed is written as a starter job, but the Antithesis threat can scale.

By default, the hidden nest has produced a few juvenile Model 3s. That is enough for a tense starter scenario, especially if civilians, evacuation, bad visibility, and faction conflict are active at the same time.

Some GMs may want a harder climax. The module now supports optional adolescent and adult Model 1 / Model 3 tokens for that purpose.

## Player durability note

Hope//Punk characters may look fragile if judged only by HP. They are harder to kill in practice because they can be wounded twice and only die on the third wound. Each time they are wounded, they restore to full health.

That means a starter group can sometimes survive more pressure than a first glance at HP suggests.

Use that durability to create desperate heroic scenes, not to grind the party down with unavoidable damage.

## Recommended baseline

For a starter group, use:

```text
1–3 Juvenile Model 3s across the scenario
Model 1s as evidence, background, or late pressure
one hidden nest hazard/objective
many civilians to protect
```

A single Juvenile Model 3 should not always fight to the death. It should try to grab biomass and escape.

## Optional boss fight: Adolescent Model 3

For a stronger climax, add one Adolescent Model 3 as the hidden nest’s first major defender.

Use this when:

```text
there are 4+ PCs
the players are tactically competent
the PCs have already ascended or are about to
the group wants a clear boss fight
NightCrash is available only as emergency extraction, not solution
```

Good placement:

```text
C12 Hidden Maintenance Cavity
C6 HVAC / Air Handling
C5 Water Recycling
D9 Service Bypass if the fight moves vertically
```

Boss-fight objective ideas:

- hold it off while civilians evacuate
- stop it from dragging Narin or another victim into the nest
- destroy the seed clump while it protects the nest
- force it into a sterilization choke point
- survive long enough for a newly ascended Samurai to act

## Adult Model 3

Use an Adult Model 3 only if the GM wants a much more dangerous scenario.

Good reasons:

- larger party
- combat-heavy table
- late-campaign revisit of Signal Bleed
- failed containment after the starter job
- “bad ending” escalation if the hidden nest is ignored

For a true starter session, an Adult Model 3 should usually be foreshadowing or a timer, not the expected fight.

Example:

```text
The adult is not fully emerged.
The PCs must prevent it from finishing.
```

## Model 1 escalation

Model 1s are the clue that the second nest exists. They are also a possible timer.

Use:

```text
juvenile Model 1 evidence in relay footage
dead Model 1 seed clump in the hidden nest
adolescent/adult Model 1 tokens only if the nest matures or the GM wants a swarm scene
```

Model 1s work best as:

- motion in vents
- swarm pressure
- aerial harassment
- evidence that the nest is producing again
- a “contain this now” warning

## Suggested scaling by group

### 2–3 PCs

- one Juvenile Model 3 at a time
- no adolescent unless heavily wounded or objective-based
- NightCrash available as evacuation safety valve
- focus on rescue and containment

### 4 PCs

- one or two Juvenile Model 3s
- optional Adolescent Model 3 as boss
- civilians and faction complications active

### 5+ PCs

- two Juvenile Model 3s plus one Adolescent Model 3
- or one Adolescent Model 3 with Model 1 swarm pressure
- multiple simultaneous objectives

## Boss-fight design rule

Do not make the climax only “reduce HP to zero.”

Better objectives:

```text
destroy the Model 1 seed clump
save a victim before they are absorbed
hold a corridor for three rounds
restore elevator power
seal the vent network
force corp and Redline to stop shooting
broadcast the relay while under attack
```

```text
model 3 adolescent.png
```
