# Missing Person Descriptions

Use these as GM-facing descriptions, witness summaries, and search-result snippets. They are written so each missing person can be introduced through rumors, posters, camera lookups, staff conversations, or Redline reports.

## Oskar Venn

**Public description:**  
Oskar Venn is a square-shouldered maintenance worker in his late forties, with a shaved head, grey stubble, heavy work gloves, and the permanent squint of someone used to broken lighting. His orange maintenance vest is patched with old union stickers and hand-written tool labels.

**How people describe him:**  
“Careful. Stubborn. Complains about everything, but fixes it anyway.”

**Last known clothing:**  
Orange maintenance vest, dark undershirt, grey utility trousers, tool belt, scuffed magnetic boots.

**Last seen:**  
C6 HVAC / Air Handling.

**Found clues:**

- tool cart left in the wrong place
- radio log: “Something is breathing in here.”
- access badge used once after disappearance
- vent cover bent outward
- drag marks toward maintenance cavity
- one boot found near a warm vent junction

**What really happened:**  
A juvenile Model 3 ambushed Oskar near the HVAC junction and dragged him toward the hidden nest. He is probably dead unless the GM wants an early rescue possibility.

## Laleh “Lala” Mir

**Public description:**  
Lala Mir is a food-distribution volunteer in her twenties or early thirties, usually seen with a bright scarf, delivery harness, and a stack of meal crates balanced like she has done this every day of her life. She has quick hands, a quicker smile, and a habit of remembering who needs extra food without asking publicly.

**How people describe her:**  
“She feeds people before they have to beg.”

**Last known clothing:**  
Teal scarf, volunteer jacket, patched cargo trousers, meal-crate harness.

**Last seen:**  
B10 Back Service Corridor, moving between aid distribution and service routes.

**Found clues:**

- spilled meal crates
- torn food packets, not eaten normally
- a child heard “dog feet in the wall”
- service hatch with acid-scored screws
- Redline supply marks near the route

**What really happened:**  
A juvenile Model 3 caught her near the service hatch and dragged her toward Map C. Her disappearance links Mara’s aid network to the hidden nest trail.

## Bex Aranda

**Public description:**  
Bex Aranda is a lean Redline Choir lookout with sharp cheekbones, chipped black nail paint, a red scarf tied at one wrist, and a cheap pistol they carry like they are trying to look more dangerous than they feel. They are young enough to be reckless and old enough to know better.

**How people describe them:**  
“Mouthy, scared of nothing, scared of Mara, loyal when it counts.”

**Last known clothing:**  
Dark jacket, red wrist scarf, black work boots, cheap sidearm, Redline token.

**Last seen:**  
A13 Service Corridor / C1 Service Street.

**Found clues:**

- dropped Redline token or knife
- scrape marks leading upward toward a service gap
- corrupted Switch footage showing something low and fast
- low-angle blood smear or impact mark
- no evidence Bex left voluntarily

**What really happened:**  
Bex did not desert. A juvenile Model 3 took them. Proving this gives Mara a direct reason to cooperate.

## Narin Pell

**Public description:**  
Narin Pell is an undocumented shelter resident in their thirties, thin, watchful, and careful about every doorway. They keep their belongings folded into a single fabric bag and speak softly unless they are helping someone else avoid attention.

**How people describe them:**  
“They survived by not being noticed. That’s why someone should have noticed when they vanished.”

**Last known clothing:**  
Brown coat, soft shoes, threadbare scarf, cloth shoulder bag.

**Last seen:**  
B8 Shelter Dorm / B12 Emergency Stair.

**Found clues:**

- bag still under bunk
- Sister Luma insists Narin would never leave it
- organic smear near stair landing
- muffled scream dismissed as a fight
- possible trail toward C11 / C12

**What really happened:**  
Narin may still be alive if the PCs move quickly. Use them as a rescue timer: the longer the PCs delay, the more likely Narin becomes part of the nest.

## Halden Rook

**Public description:**  
Halden Rook was a corporate cleanup technician attached to the original nest sterilization operation. He was narrow-faced, precise, and looked more like a lab-safety officer than a soldier. His ID photo shows a man trying very hard not to look frightened.

**How people describe him:**  
“Annoying because he read the procedures. Dangerous because he noticed when the procedures were wrong.”

**Last known clothing:**  
Corporate cleanup suit, sealed helmet, hazard tag, chest-mounted recorder.

**Last seen:**  
Old nest cleanup site / D6 Control Room records.

**Found clues:**

- final annotation: “They’re not fleeing. They’re relocating.”
- deleted warning: “nest-seeding risk”
- overruled report classification: “terminal erratic flight”
- relay copy of his helmet feed
- personnel file marked reassigned instead of dead

**What really happened:**  
Halden understood that the Model 1s were not fleeing randomly. His warning was buried because admitting it would mean the cleanup had failed.

## How to present missing-person evidence

Start mundane. Do not reveal Antithesis immediately.

Good first descriptions:

- “No-show patient.”
- “Maintenance worker missing after a vent call.”
- “Food volunteer did not come back.”
- “Gang lookout deserted.”
- “Shelter resident left without their bag.”

Then sharpen the pattern:

- all routes touch service infrastructure
- all disappearances happen near ducts, stairs, or back corridors
- camera footage is missing, edited, or shows low fast movement
- there are drag marks and biological residue
- people heard clicking, dog feet, or breathing in the walls
