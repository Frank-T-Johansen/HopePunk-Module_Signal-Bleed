# Recovered Notebook from B8 Isolation Ward

This notebook is hidden behind a loose wall panel in the sealed isolation ward on Floor B.

The cover is smoke-stained and partly melted. Most identifying marks have been scraped off. Several pages are missing. The handwriting begins neat and clinical, then becomes rushed, uneven, and harder to read.

Inside the back cover, someone has written:

> If they come back, do not let them say this was an accident.

## At a glance

The notebook appears to describe a covert biological research operation run inside the ward under false medical cover.

The writer never names the corporation directly, but the language, equipment references, security procedures, and cleanup orders all point toward a well-funded corporate program.

The entries describe:

- nonhuman biological samples,
- deliberate growth and feeding trials,
- staff ordered not to use the word **Antithesis**,
- small flying forms later called **Model 1s**,
- a containment breach,
- deaths among researchers and staff,
- a corporate powered-armor cleanup team,
- and at least several small forms escaping before the ward was burned out.

## Selected readable entries

### Day 1

Ward B8 has been sealed under emergency corporate authority.

Officially, this is a biohazard preparedness exercise. That is what the clinic-facing staff were told. It is not true.

Specimens arrived in armored refrigeration cases under guard. No proper chain of custody. No external logging. No names on the transport manifest, only internal asset numbers.

We were told the material is nonhuman, high-risk, and not to be discussed outside the ward.

No one has said the word aloud yet.

Everyone is thinking it.

### Day 2

The tissue is not inert.

It responds to heat, electrical stimulation, and nutrient exposure. It does not merely twitch. It reorganizes.

When the tray warms, it thickens toward the heat source. When exposed to animal tissue, it grows faster. When the room quiets, the smaller structures shift toward the glass.

I requested confirmation that this material came from a sterilized battle site.

No answer.

### Day 3

The project lead says we are studying early growth behavior for “containment response.”

That is the phrase used in front of guards.

In private, one of the corporate observers called it “battlefield applicability.”

The distinction is moral theater. Everyone here knows it.

### Day 4

We began controlled feeding trials.

I objected. The objection was logged, then ignored.

The material consumed everything placed in the enclosure. It formed chambers, ridges, and a warm central mass. It is becoming organized.

One of the younger researchers said it looks like a nest.

The project lead told her not to use that word again.

### Day 5

Containment breach, minor.

A drone arm was caught through the access hatch. The mass wrapped it, cracked the casing, and incorporated pieces into the surrounding structure.

Not as machinery. As scaffolding.

I no longer believe we are observing a sample.

I believe we are helping it build itself.

### Day 6

Small autonomous forms emerged from the mass.

Fast. Fragile. Airborne.

Security called them **Model 1s** before anyone in the room had assigned a name.

That means corporate has seen these before.

This was never exploratory research. They knew enough to classify the forms before they appeared.

### Day 7

The ward smells wrong.

The vents pulse with warm air even when the system is off. The walls near the inner partition are damp. The smallest forms hide in dark recesses when disturbed, then return when the room quiets.

Two staff requested removal from the project.

One was escorted out by security.

Her access profile was deleted within the hour.

### Day 8

Containment failure.

I do not know how many are dead.

At least three Model 1s escaped before the shutters came down. Larger low-bodied forms followed from the inner growth. Their jaws opened in three directions.

Technician Vale was pulled under a gurney. I saw the suit tear at the neck.

The alarms failed room by room.

Then the cameras failed.

### Day 8, later

Rusk sealed the ward.

Not to save us.

To contain the evidence.

### Day 9

They came in wearing powered armor.

Corporate retrieval, not clinic security. Full seal. Incendiaries. Heavy weapons. No rescue protocol.

They burned the growth. Burned the bodies. Burned anything organic they could not identify in seconds.

One of them said:

> Primary nest neutralized.

Another answered:

> Sweep for strays and pull every record.

They think they ended it here.

They are wrong.

I saw at least two small flying forms escape through the service gap before the fire teams locked the ward down.

If those things found somewhere dark and hidden, this is not over.

## Final page

This was deliberate.

They brought alien material here.  
They fed it.  
They studied it.  
They lost control.  
Then they erased the people involved.

Look for:

- burn marks under fresh wall coating,
- replaced wall panels,
- sealed service gaps,
- residue in drainage traps,
- missing staff records,
- deleted camera footage,
- and flight-vector notes buried in the cleanup logs.

If the small ones escaped, B8 was only the beginning.

Do not trust anyone who says the danger is over.
