# Surveillance, Cameras, and Ad-Injection Systems

## Default assumption

In this megacomplex, cameras are almost everywhere.

Do not ask “is there a camera here?” for every room. Assume there probably is.

Instead, track:

```text
- where cameras are missing
- who controls the camera feed
- how difficult the feed is to access
- whether the feed has been edited, looped, blocked, or monetized
```

## Camera ownership categories

Use these ownership labels in GM notes:

```text
Clinic-owned
Redline-controlled
Corporate-compromised
Megacomplex municipal
Ad-network / sponsor-owned
Dead / blind spot
Unknown / contested
```

## Hacking difficulty guidance

Use these as rough difficulty anchors:

```text
Difficulty 8:
Obvious public camera, badly secured, old system, or staff willingly helps.

Difficulty 10:
Normal clinic/internal camera access with cooperation or basic credentials.

Difficulty 12:
Restricted clinic/security camera, Redline camera, or municipal feed.

Difficulty 14:
Corporate-compromised camera, edited logs, or active countermeasures.

Difficulty 16:
Live corporate recovery overwatch, hardened control room, or hostile trace risk.

Difficulty 18+:
Alien-corrupted signal, Samurai-level sponsor feed, or active ICE with consequences.
```

Failures should usually create complications, not dead ends:

- trace starts
- feed freezes
- false footage appears
- ads inject into cyberware
- a control-room NPC notices
- the wrong faction learns someone is watching

## Camera control by map

### Floor A — Mercy Twelve Clinic

Default: Clinic-owned.

Exceptions:

- public concourse cameras are mostly municipal/ad-network
- Redline Choir has control or partial access around side corridors and some street approaches
- emergency intake bay cameras were looped for six minutes
- staff office / records has limited cameras for privacy
- pediatric/community corner has cameras, but staff are protective about who can access them
- recovery wing cameras are privacy-restricted and hard to access without Dr. Valez or staff cooperation

### Floor B — Community Support / Shelter

Default: mixed clinic/community control.

Exceptions:

- public street cameras are municipal/ad-network
- food distribution and service corridor have Redline-controlled blind spots
- classroom and daycare cameras are restricted; staff will resist casual access
- counselor offices have no ordinary camera coverage, only door logs
- shelter dorm cameras are limited and controversial

### Floor C — Utility / Service

Default: municipal/maintenance control.

Exceptions:

- Redline Choir has access to some older service cameras
- corporate recovery may compromise freight lift and service street feeds
- hidden maintenance bypasses often have dead cameras
- signal interference node may corrupt footage

### Floor D — Quarantine / Incident

Default: restricted clinic/corporate hybrid control.

Exceptions:

- quarantine rooms have internal monitoring but may be locked behind emergency protocol
- control room can see most of the floor
- landing corner has municipal/emergency-response cameras
- NightCrash may have a sponsor/emergency bodycam feed if she arrives
- alien breach can corrupt camera feeds into impossible images

## Ad-injection and cyber-eye harassment

Megacomplex advertising is aggressive. Cheap cyberware often subsidizes itself through ad-based revenue.

Use this sparingly as color, pressure, and occasional complication.

### Where ad-injection is strongest

- public concourse
- elevator lobby
- transit access
- support center public commons
- municipal camera zones
- sponsor-owned clinic signage
- landing corner emergency beacons

### What happens

Characters with cyber-eyes, cheap AR, sponsorware, or old background cyberware may experience:

- clinic ads overlaid on real patients
- “helpful” arrows pointing to paid services
- sponsor slogans appearing in peripheral vision
- emotional manipulation overlays
- fake emergency warnings
- pop-up coupons for painkillers, trauma foam, or therapy apps
- adware trying to tag what the PC looks at

### Suggested checks

- **Cyberware / Mesh Hacker:** block or trace the ad injection.
- **Awareness:** realize a visual prompt is an overlay, not reality.
- **Corporate Security:** recognize sponsor-network surveillance behavior.
- **Psychology:** notice the ad is tuned to panic, grief, or pain.
- **Performance:** hijack the ad system for public messaging.
- **Grease Monkey:** physically disable a local ad beacon.

### Difficulty

```text
Difficulty 8:
Annoying public ad overlay.

Difficulty 10:
Targeted adware trying to profile the PC.

Difficulty 12:
Cyber-eye overlay interfering with perception.

Difficulty 14:
Hostile ad injection during crisis, possibly corporate-assisted.

Difficulty 16:
Alien signal or corporate ICE piggybacks through the ad layer.
```

## Human control rooms

Both the clinic and the Redline Choir have people watching feeds.

They should be NPC interaction points, not just hacking targets.

### Clinic control room

The clinic control room is under-resourced but legitimate. Staff may cooperate if the PCs have earned trust.

They can provide:

- camera lookups
- door logs
- patient movement records
- elevator use
- emergency intake feed
- partial recovery-wing access with privacy limits

### Redline control room

The Redline Choir control room is unofficial, patched together, and better than outsiders expect.

They can provide:

- street approach feeds
- side corridor blind spots
- gang lookout reports
- who entered by unofficial routes
- local witness names
- hidden supply-route footage

They will not cooperate if treated like criminals only.

## Suggested camera-related clue philosophy

Camera access should help players ask better questions.

Do not let cameras replace play.

Good camera results:

- “The courier did not come through the emergency bay.”
- “The camera froze six minutes before the relay arrived.”
- “Bluewire was already shaking before the argument.”
- “A Redline courier delivered medical crates, not weapons.”
- “A corporate observer has been in the concourse for an hour.”
- “One corridor has no camera because someone deliberately keeps it blind.”

Bad camera results:

- “Here is the entire plot.”
- “You solve every mystery without leaving the room.”
