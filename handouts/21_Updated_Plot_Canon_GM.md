# Signal Bleed: Updated Plot Canon

## One-sentence premise

Signal Bleed is about a stolen human data relay proving that a corporation’s illegal Antithesis bio-research created a nest, that their cleanup failed, and that a second hidden nest is now forming beneath the community they came to silence.

## Hard setting constraints

```text
The relay is human technology.
The relay is evidence, not alien technology.
The relay does not summon Antithesis.
The Antithesis threat is biological, not electronic.
Cheap cyberware ad-injection is human exploitation, not alien interference.
Samurai AI communications are not hacked, intercepted, copied, or analyzed.
```

## What happened before the PCs arrive

The corporation gathered Antithesis biological matter from previous battle sites and stored it in a hidden megacomplex research annex. They thought they were studying inert or controllable samples.

They were wrong.

The stored biomass consumed researchers and lab staff, incorporated them as material, and formed a small nest. That first nest produced Model 1s, the small flying Antithesis.

Corporate containment forces attacked the visible nest and nearly exterminated it. The official internal conclusion was that the Antithesis event was contained.

But several Model 1s behaved unexpectedly. They did not attack, scatter, or die in place. They flew through ducts and hidden service routes to a nearby concealed location, then suicided in a clump. Their bodies seeded a second, smaller, hidden nest.

The corporation does not know this second nest exists.

## What the corporation thinks is happening now

Corporate Recovery believes the old nest is dead or contained. Their current mission is not a rescue operation. They are at Mercy Twelve to clean up evidence and witnesses.

Their objectives:

```text
Recover or destroy the stolen relay.
Find the courier.
Suppress clinic records.
Prevent Redline Choir from broadcasting the evidence.
Identify witnesses.
Blame gang violence, panic, or rogue staff if needed.
Avoid public evacuation unless corporate liability can be controlled.
```

Commander Rusk can honestly believe the original nest was destroyed while still lying about the experiments and witness cleanup.

## What is actually happening now

The second hidden nest is already active.

It has produced a few juvenile Model 3s. These are dog-like Antithesis predators with jaws that split into three parts. They hunt isolated biomass, kill or disable it, then drag it back to the nest so the nest can grow more Antithesis.

The missing-person pattern is the first visible sign that the cleanup failed.

## The Quill Relay

The relay is a human data-core stolen by Tamsin Quill.

It contains:

```text
patient lists
illegal trial records
staff orders
dosage records
researcher rosters
quarantine logs
camera footage
shipping manifests
Antithesis sample inventory
corporate cleanup orders
evidence that locals were used as deniable subjects
footage of Model 1s behaving strangely during cleanup
a buried warning that the Model 1 flight vector was not random
```

The relay’s most important clue is initially easy to miss:

```text
Several Model 1s escaped the destroyed nest and flew somewhere else.
```

The corporation dismissed this as terminal erratic behavior.

A dead or missing researcher flagged it as nest-seeding risk before being overruled.

## What “Signal Bleed” means

Signal Bleed is not literal alien electronics corruption.

The title means:

```text
The truth is leaking.
The evidence is spreading.
The corporation cannot contain the story.
```

In-world, characters may use “signal bleed” to describe the relay’s copied evidence appearing in clinic terminals, camera buffers, student slates, and Redline mesh caches. That is human data leakage and human hacking, not Antithesis influence.

## Main scenario arc

1. **The courier arrives:** Tamsin Quill reaches Mercy Twelve with the relay and collapses.
2. **Human pressure:** the PCs deal with clinic staff, Redline Choir, Bluewire, civilians, corporate observers, surveillance gaps, and the first missing-person clues.
3. **The old nest:** the relay reveals that corporate Antithesis bio-research created a nest. Researchers died. Model 1s were produced. Corporate sterilization destroyed the visible nest.
4. **The wrong conclusion:** footage shows Model 1s flying away in formation. Corporate reports call this “terminal erratic flight.”
5. **The missing people:** the PCs connect missing maintenance workers, food volunteers, Redline lookouts, shelter residents, and drag marks to a second hidden nest.
6. **The hidden nest:** the PCs discover juvenile Model 3s dragging biomass back to the nest.
7. **First Samurai ascension:** the PCs protect civilians under impossible pressure.
8. **Aftermath:** NightCrash arrives as emergency extraction, field recognition, or post-victory contact.

## Core GM mystery question

```text
Where did the escaped Model 1s go?
```

The player-facing question starts as:

```text
Who gets the relay?
```

Then becomes:

```text
What was the corporation hiding?
```

Then becomes:

```text
Why are people disappearing if the nest was supposedly destroyed?
```
